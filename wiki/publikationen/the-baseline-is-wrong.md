## Kernthesen
- The EU's reformed fiscal rules make Debt Sustainability Analyses (DSAs) the central steering tool for fiscal policy, yet current DSAs largely ignore both climate damages and the growth impact of policies needed to meet the EU's emissions constraint.
- Climate change is already committing the global economy to substantial income losses (11 to 29 percent until 2050 according to Kotz et al. 2024); for Europe damages may reduce output by 5 to 10 percent by 2050.
- Reaching the EU's 2030 target of at least 55 percent emission reduction primarily via carbon pricing reduces GDP — EC Impact Assessment estimates (ETS2) range from -0.7 to -0.3 percentage points of GDP in 2030, while broader literature (NGFS, OECD) suggests roughly 1 to 2 percent GDP loss by the mid-2030s.
- A balanced policy mix that combines (lower) carbon prices with targeted public climate investment likely produces better growth and fiscal outcomes than a pure carbon-pricing strategy, particularly in the presence of market failures and uncoordinated global action.
- Including climate damages and policy-mix-dependent growth in the DSA baseline can change debt-to-GDP projections materially (for Italy in 2028: EC baseline 146 → climate-adjusted baseline 148 percent from damages plus policy adjustment; standard policy mix with multiplier 0.75 raises this further to 149 percent — PDF S. 12, 17).

## Schlussfolgerungen
- DSA methodology must be reformed so that baseline growth projections become endogenous to climate policy choices and incorporate the EU's legally binding emissions constraint, see also [[ausgaben-fuer-die-dekarbonisierung]] and [[klimaausgaben-in-schuldenregel]].
- Fiscal multipliers used in DSAs should be refined to reflect the higher and longer-lasting multipliers found for green public investment, rather than the conventional Commission assumption of 0.75 with only a short-run impact.
- Model assumptions must be aligned across climate and fiscal frameworks to reduce inconsistencies; NGFS scenarios were not designed for DSA use and require careful adjustment.
