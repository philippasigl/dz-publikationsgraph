## Kernthesen
- The ECB's current monetary policy strategy is unduly myopic: by stabilising inflation only over a two- to five-year horizon and relying almost exclusively on demand-focused interest-rate adjustments, it fails to detect structural risks to price stability before they materialise.
- The 2022-23 inflation surge was driven by supply-side disruptions, corporate pricing power and climate-related shocks – risks that lie outside the ECB's existing analytical framework and that well-anchored inflation expectations proved ineffective at suppressing.
- Aggressive rate hikes brought inflation down but at clear cost: they raised funding costs indiscriminately, undermined investment in clean energy and made long-term price stability more vulnerable to future shocks – clean energy is interest-rate-sensitive because most of its costs are paid upfront.
- The EU's dollar dependence is a structural inflation risk: 50% of EU imports and an estimated 85% of EU energy imports are denominated in dollars, while only 41% are in euro, exposing prices to dollar movements (the euro lost almost 20% against the dollar between Q4 2021 and Q2 2022 while energy prices rose 41.5%).
- The legal mandate of the ECB was deliberately left open by its drafters and offers ample room to adapt the strategy to new structural challenges – the 2025 review is the moment to do so.

## Schlussfolgerungen
- Broaden the time horizon of the ECB's monetary policy strategy so that the long-term preconditions of price stability become part of the primary price-stability objective.
- Create a third analytical pillar dedicated to long-term and structural risks – climate change, energy dependence, demographics, market power and geopolitical disruptions – alongside the existing economic and monetary pillars.
- Embed the ECB's strategy within a wider EU inflation governance framework that supports strategic coordination with fiscal, industrial and competition policies – see also [Hawkish Winds: Wie Zinsen die Energiewende treffen](/wiki/publikationen/hawkish-winds-wie-zinsen-die-energiewende-treffen) and [Geldpolitische Implementierung im Wandel](/wiki/publikationen/geldpolitische-implementierung-im-wandel).
